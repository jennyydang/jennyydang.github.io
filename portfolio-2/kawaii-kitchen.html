Jenny Dang - Kawaii Kitchen

Kawaii Kitchen

UX Design + Branding

"Kawaii Kitchen is a cooking-themed board game that teaches children to learn to concentration on identifying images and interacting with other players. Kawaii Kitchen is targeted to children ages 4 - 7 with Asperger's Syndrome, but anybody older than the age 7 or without Asperger's Syndrome can play as well!

The purpose of Kawaii Kitchen is not only to keep children occupied, but to build their concentration, learn to identify/read (ingredients), and cooperate with other players."

Research

  • Monotonous behaviors
  • Difficult to communicate and interact with others in a social situation
  • Excessively focused on a certain topic or object
  • Conditions that affects one's ability to behave socially in certain settings like work, school, and etc.

Symbol

The rabbit is seen as sensitive, kind and passive creatures. I chose the rabbit to be the mascot because not only is it cute, but I wanted to make a soft familiar looking animal that children can identify and connect with right away.

Signature

I wanted a font that looked "cute and bubbly." When I was little, I used to write in bubble words, so I wanted to incorporate that writing style into the logo. The fonts I ended up using were: DK Borubu (Kawaii) and Giddyup (Kitchen).

Color

I chose to use the rainbow style colors because it stands out and it would remind the children of rainbows = fun/happiness. It has both strong and calming colors. The colors in my previous designs were mainly calming colors such as blue and purple, but I decided to change it because the colors were too relaxing and boring to look at.

User Personas

The Connectivity Model allows me to observe and analyze the users' experience. By watching sample youtube videos of children with Aseperger's Syndrome playing board games, I was able to observe their social, emotional, and behavioral actions. With this information, I can apply the information I collected to my very own board game.
